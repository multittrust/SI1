Call for contributions

Background and Scope

This special issue, inspired by the International Workshops on Multidisciplinary Perspectives on Human-AI Team Trust (MultiTTrust), delves into the dynamics of trust within human-AI teams. It seeks to explore complex interactions and trust-building mechanisms in these collaborations, addressing the rapidly evolving field of AI in societal and technological contexts.

We welcome submissions from a diverse range of fields, including but not limited to:

  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Cognitive Science and Psychology
  • Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)
  • Robotics and Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
  • Computer Science and Engineering
  • Social Sciences and Ethics

For detailed topics please refer to the topics section.

Submission Guidelines

Submissions should adhere to a minimum length of 8 pages and a maximum of 20 pages (excluding references), approximately corresponding to 3,600 to 8,000 words, to ensure comprehensive coverage and maintain conciseness. Moreover, submissions based on previous workshop papers must include significant expansion in terms of content, depth, and scope, with new material to be considered for publication. For aspects not explicitly outlined in our call, we will adhere to the submission guidelines of 'Interaction Studies', encompassing standard protocols for formatting, manuscript structuring, and the overall submission process.

Review Process

The review process will begin with an initial screening by the guest editors to ensure each submission aligns with the special issue's scope and thematic focus. Once deemed suitable, manuscripts will undergo a peer-review process, involving independent reviews by experts in the field. These reviews will evaluate the manuscript for its academic rigor, contribution to the field, and adherence to the special issue's themes. Based on these reviews, the outcomes for each manuscript may include: acceptance, acceptance with minor revisions, revise and resubmit pending major revisions, or outright rejection. The final decision on manuscript acceptance will be based on the reviewers' recommendations and the guest editors' judgment, ensuring a fair and comprehensive assessment of each submission.
